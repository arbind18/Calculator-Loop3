Understanding Metabolic Syndrome

What is Metabolic Syndrome?

Metabolic syndrome is a cluster of conditions that occur together, increasing your risk of heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes. These conditions include increased blood pressure, high blood sugar, excess body fat around the waist, and abnormal cholesterol or triglyceride levels.

The 5 Criteria

According to the NCEP ATP III guidelines, metabolic syndrome is diagnosed if you have 3 or more of the following 5 risk factors:

  • Abdominal Obesity: Waist circumference > 40 inches (men) or > 35 inches (women).
  • High Triglycerides: 150 mg/dL or higher.
  • Low HDL Cholesterol: < 40 mg/dL (men) or < 50 mg/dL (women).
  • High Blood Pressure: 130/85 mmHg or higher.
  • High Fasting Glucose: 100 mg/dL or higher.

Prevention and Management

Weight Loss: Losing 7-10% of your body weight can reduce insulin resistance and blood pressure.

Exercise: Get at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise, such as brisk walking, daily.

Healthy Diet: Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein. Limit sugar, salt, and saturated fats.

Metabolic Syndrome: Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is metabolic syndrome reversible?

Yes, metabolic syndrome can often be reversed with lifestyle changes. Weight loss, exercise, and a healthy diet can improve all 5 components of the syndrome.

2. What causes metabolic syndrome?

It is closely linked to overweight or obesity and inactivity. It is also linked to a condition called insulin resistance. Genetics and age also play a role.

3. Why is it dangerous?

Having metabolic syndrome can increase your risk of developing type 2 diabetes by 5 times and your risk of heart attack or stroke by 2 times compared to people without the syndrome.
