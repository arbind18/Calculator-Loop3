How It Works

The cross product of two vectors $\mathbf{a} = (a_x, a_y, a_z)$ and $\mathbf{b} = (b_x, b_y, b_z)$ results in a new vector $\mathbf{c}$ that is perpendicular to both $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$.

The formula is derived from the determinant of a matrix:

$$ \mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ a_x & a_y & a_z \\ b_x & b_y & b_z \end{vmatrix} $$

Expanding this determinant gives:

$$ \mathbf{c} = (a_y b_z - a_z b_y)\mathbf{i} - (a_x b_z - a_z b_x)\mathbf{j} + (a_x b_y - a_y b_x)\mathbf{k} $$

The magnitude $|\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b}|$ represents the area of the parallelogram formed by the two vectors.
