How It Works

The inverse of a matrix $A$ is denoted as $A^{-1}$, such that $A \times A^{-1} = I$ (the identity matrix).

The inverse exists only if the determinant $\det(A)$ is non-zero.

2x2 Matrix

For $A = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$:

$$ A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc} \begin{pmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{pmatrix} $$

3x3 Matrix

For a 3x3 matrix, the inverse is calculated using the adjugate matrix:

$$ A^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(A)} \text{adj}(A) $$

Where $\text{adj}(A)$ is the transpose of the cofactor matrix.
