Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What is ISO week number?
A: ISO week number is a standardized way (ISO 8601) to number weeks of the year from 1-53. Week 1 is the first week with at least 4 days in the new year, and weeks start on Monday.
Q2: How do I find today's week number?
A: Simply click the "Today" button for instant results, or enter today's date manually and click Calculate.
Q3: Why do some years have 53 weeks?
A: A year has 53 weeks when January 1st falls on Thursday, or it's a leap year starting on Wednesday. This happens approximately every 5-6 years.
Q4: What's the difference between ISO week and calendar week?
A: ISO weeks always start on Monday and belong to the year with the most days in that week. Calendar weeks may start on Sunday and simply count weeks from January 1.
Q5: Can week 1 start in December?
A: Yes! If January 1-3 fall on Friday-Sunday, those days belong to the last week (52 or 53) of the previous year. Week 1 then starts on the first Monday.
Q6: Is this calculator accurate for all years?
A: Yes! Our calculator uses the standard ISO 8601 algorithm and is accurate for any year from 1900 to 2100.
Q7: What is "week of month"?
A: Week of month shows which week within the current month (1-5). It's calculated from the first day of the month, counting weeks that start on Monday.
Q8: Can I use this for payroll?
A: Yes! Many companies use ISO week numbers for payroll. However, confirm your company follows ISO 8601 standards before using for official purposes.
Q9: Is the calculator free to use?

Q10: Does it work offline?
