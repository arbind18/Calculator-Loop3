The 50/30/20 Rule Explained

Senator Elizabeth Warren popularized the 50/30/20 rule of budgeting in her book "All Your Worth: The Ultimate Lifetime Money Plan."

  • 50% Needs: Bills that you absolutely must pay and are necessary for survival. Examples: rent/mortgage, car payments, groceries, insurance, health care, minimum debt payments, and utilities.
  • 30% Wants: All the things you spend money on that are not absolutely essential. Examples: dinner and movies out, tickets to sporting events, vacations, the latest electronics gadget, and ultra-high-speed internet.
  • 20% Savings: Money allocated to savings accounts, debt repayment (above minimums), and investments.
