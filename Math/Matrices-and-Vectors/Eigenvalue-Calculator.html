How It Works

Eigenvalues ($\lambda$) are scalars associated with a linear system of equations (i.e., a matrix equation) that are sometimes also known as characteristic roots.

They are found by solving the characteristic equation:

$$ \det(A - \lambda I) = 0 $$

2x2 Matrix

For a 2x2 matrix, this leads to a quadratic equation:

$$ \lambda^2 - \text{tr}(A)\lambda + \det(A) = 0 $$

Where $\text{tr}(A)$ is the trace (sum of diagonal elements) and $\det(A)$ is the determinant.
