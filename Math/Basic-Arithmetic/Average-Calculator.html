Understanding Averages

In statistics, an "average" is a number that represents the central tendency of a data set. The most common types are:

  • Mean: The sum of all values divided by the number of values.
  • Median: The middle value when the data is ordered from least to greatest.
  • Mode: The value that appears most frequently in the data set.

Standard Deviation & Variance

These metrics measure the spread or dispersion of the data:

σ = √[ Σ(x - μ)² / N ]

Standard Deviation (σ): Indicates how much the data points deviate from the mean. A low standard deviation means data points are close to the mean.
Variance (σ²): The average of the squared differences from the mean.

Quick Tips

  • Separate numbers with commas or spaces.
  • Copy-paste data from Excel or Sheets.
  • Check the histogram for data distribution.
  • Outliers can significantly affect the mean.
