Understanding Volume Calculation

Volume is the quantity of three-dimensional space enclosed by a closed surface, for example, the space that a substance (solid, liquid, gas, or plasma) or shape occupies or contains.

Common Volume Formulas

Cube

A cube is a three-dimensional solid object bounded by six square faces, facets or sides, with three meeting at each vertex.

Volume = Side³

Cylinder

A cylinder is a three-dimensional solid, one of the most basic curvilinear geometric shapes.

Volume = π × r² × h

Sphere

A sphere is a geometrical object in three-dimensional space that is the surface of a ball.

Volume = (4/3) × π × r³

Cone

A cone is a three-dimensional geometric shape that tapers smoothly from a flat base to a point called the apex or vertex.

Volume = (1/3) × π × r² × h

Rectangular Box (Cuboid)

A cuboid is a convex polyhedron bounded by six quadrilateral faces.

Volume = Length × Width × Height

Applications of Volume Calculation

  • Shipping: Calculating the volume of packages for shipping costs.
  • Construction: Estimating concrete needed for a foundation.
  • Chemistry: Measuring liquid volumes for reactions.
  • Water Management: Calculating the capacity of tanks or reservoirs.
