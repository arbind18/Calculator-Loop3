About Excavation Calculation

Excavation calculations are critical for construction projects to estimate the amount of earth that needs to be moved and the associated costs. Understanding the difference between "Bank Volume" and "Loose Volume" is essential.

Key Concepts

  • Bank Volume: The volume of the soil in its natural state before excavation.
  • Loose Volume: The volume of the soil after it has been excavated. Soil expands when dug up due to air pockets being introduced. This expansion is known as "swell".
  • Swell Factor: The percentage increase in volume from bank state to loose state.

Common Swell Factors

  • Sand: 10-15%
  • Topsoil: 20-25%
  • Clay: 30-40%
  • Gravel: 10-15%
  • Rock (Blasted): 50-80%
