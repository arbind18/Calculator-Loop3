How can I protect my savings from inflation?

Invest in assets that beat inflation: Equity mutual funds (12-15% returns), real estate (6-10%), gold (8-10%), PPF (7-7.5%). Avoid keeping too much in savings accounts (3-4% interest loses to 5-6% inflation). Rule: Keep only 3-6 months expenses in savings, invest rest. Diversify across asset classes for balance of growth and safety.

Is gold a good hedge against inflation?

Yes, gold historically preserves value during inflation. Gold returns averaged 10-12% annually in India over past 20 years, beating inflation comfortably. During high inflation (>7%), gold outperforms most assets. However, don't allocate >10-15% portfolio to gold as it doesn't generate income like stocks/bonds. Digital gold, gold ETFs, or Sovereign Gold Bonds offer convenient investment without storage hassles.

Should I invest in inflation-indexed bonds?

India offers Inflation-Indexed Bonds (IIBs) and Inflation-Indexed National Savings Securities (IINSS) where returns adjust with inflation. Principal and interest increase with CPI inflation. Guaranteed real returns of 1-1.5% above inflation. Good for conservative investors seeking inflation protection. However, liquidity lower than regular bonds. Better alternatives: Equity mutual funds (higher returns), PPF (7-7.5% fixed), or balanced portfolio with stocks+bonds for growth with safety.
