CAGR Formula

CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/Number of Years) - 1
What is CAGR and how is it calculated?
CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the rate of return that would be required for an investment to grow from its beginning value to its ending value, assuming profits were reinvested. Formula: CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/Number of Years) - 1.
Is CAGR better than absolute returns?
Yes, CAGR is better for comparing investments because it normalizes returns across different time periods. A 100% return in 1 year (100% CAGR) is much better than 100% return in 10 years (7.2% CAGR). CAGR accounts for the time value of money.
What is a good CAGR for mutual funds in India?
For equity mutual funds in India, a good CAGR is 12-15% over 10+ years. Top performers achieve 15-20% CAGR. Debt funds typically deliver 6-8% CAGR, hybrid funds 9-12% CAGR. Compare fund CAGR with benchmark index - Nifty 50 has delivered ~12% CAGR historically.
Can CAGR be negative?
Yes, CAGR can be negative when your investment loses value over time. If you invested ?1,00,000 and it's now worth ?80,000 after 3 years, CAGR = -7.2% per year. Negative CAGR indicates your investment is shrinking annually.
Should I use CAGR or XIRR for SIP investments?
Use XIRR for SIP investments, not CAGR. CAGR assumes a lump sum investment, while XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return) accounts for multiple cash flows at different times - perfect for SIP. CAGR only works for one-time investments.
