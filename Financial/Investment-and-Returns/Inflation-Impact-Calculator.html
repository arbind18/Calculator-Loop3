Fisher Equation: Real Return = [(1 + Nominal Rate) / (1 + Inflation Rate)] - 1
Inflation erodes the purchasing power of money. Always consider inflation-adjusted (real) returns when planning long-term investments.

Understanding Inflation Impact

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, and subsequently, purchasing power is falling. Central banks attempt to stop severe inflation, along with severe deflation, in an attempt to keep the excessive growth of prices to a minimum.

Real Rate of Return: This is the annual percentage return realized on an investment, which is adjusted for changes in prices due to inflation or other external effects. This method expresses the nominal rate of return in real terms, which keeps the purchasing power of a given level of capital constant over time.

Why it matters: If your savings account pays 4% interest but inflation is at 6%, you are actually losing purchasing power every year. To grow your wealth, your investments must earn a return higher than the inflation rate.
