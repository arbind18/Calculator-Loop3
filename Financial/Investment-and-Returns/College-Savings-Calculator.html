Planning for Higher Education

Rising education costs make early planning essential. Our College Savings Calculator helps you estimate the future cost of a degree and determine how much you need to save monthly to reach your goal.

Key Factors Affecting Your Goal

  • Time Horizon: The earlier you start, the more time your money has to compound.
  • Inflation: Education inflation often outpaces general inflation (typically 6-8%).
  • Investment Returns: Higher returns can significantly reduce the amount you need to save out-of-pocket.

Investment Options

Consider tax-advantaged accounts like 529 Plans (in the US) or specific child education mutual funds and insurance plans available in your region.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does college cost increase per year?
Historically, college tuition costs have increased by about 5% to 8% annually, which is higher than the general inflation rate.
When should I start saving for college?
The best time to start is as soon as the child is born. Starting early allows you to take advantage of compound interest, meaning you can save less per month to reach the same goal.
