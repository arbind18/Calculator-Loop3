About ROI Calculator

Return on Investment (ROI) is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency or profitability of an investment or compare the efficiency of a number of different investments. ROI tries to directly measure the amount of return on a particular investment, relative to the investment's cost.

How to Calculate ROI?

The basic formula for ROI is:

ROI = ((Current Value of Investment - Cost of Investment) / Cost of Investment) * 100

What is Annualized ROI?

Annualized ROI, also known as CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate), takes into account the time period of the investment. It shows you the geometric progression ratio that provides a constant rate of return over the time period.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good ROI?
A "good" ROI depends on the risk tolerance and the time required for the investment to generate a return. Generally, an annual ROI of 7% or more (adjusted for inflation) is considered good for stock market investments.
What is the difference between ROI and Profit?
Profit is the absolute amount of money gained (e.g., ?5,000), while ROI is the percentage return relative to the initial investment (e.g., 50%). ROI allows for easier comparison between investments of different sizes.
Does this calculator account for inflation?
This basic ROI calculator does not automatically adjust for inflation. However, you can subtract the inflation rate from the calculated Annualized ROI to get the "Real Rate of Return".
How do I use the Annualized ROI?
Use Annualized ROI to compare investments held for different time periods. For example, a 20% total return over 1 year is much better than a 20% total return over 5 years. Annualized ROI makes this comparison clear.
