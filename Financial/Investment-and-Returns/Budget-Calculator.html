Budget Planning Guide 2025

Budget planning is the foundation of financial health. By tracking your income and expenses, you can identify spending leaks, prioritize savings, and achieve your financial goals faster. Our calculator helps you visualize your cash flow and adhere to popular budgeting frameworks like the 50/30/20 rule.

The 50/30/20 Rule

This popular budgeting method suggests allocating your after-tax income as follows:

  • 50% Needs: Essentials like rent, groceries, utilities, and minimum debt payments.
  • 30% Wants: Discretionary spending like dining out, entertainment, and hobbies.
  • 20% Savings: Savings, investments, and extra debt repayment.

How much should I save each month?
Aim to save at least 20% of your income. If you're just starting, even 10% is a great first step. As your income grows, try to increase your savings rate to accelerate wealth building.
What counts as a "Need" vs "Want"?
Needs are expenses you cannot avoid (rent, basic food, electricity). Wants are expenses you can live without (Netflix, restaurant meals, new clothes). Be honest with your categorization!
How do I handle irregular expenses?
For annual expenses like insurance or festivals, estimate the total yearly cost and divide by 12. Set aside this amount monthly in a "sinking fund" so you're prepared when the bill arrives.
