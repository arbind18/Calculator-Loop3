Complete Guide to APR Calculator 2025

Annual Percentage Rate (APR) is the true cost of borrowing money expressed as a yearly percentage. Unlike the basic interest rate, APR includes all costs associated with a loan - the interest rate, processing fees, insurance charges, administrative costs, and other mandatory fees. This makes APR the most accurate measure for comparing different loan offers.

APR vs Interest Rate

Interest rate is just the percentage charged on the principal amount, while APR includes the interest rate plus all additional fees and costs associated with the loan. APR gives you a more accurate picture of the total cost. For example, a 10% interest rate loan might have a 12.5% APR after including all fees.

What is a good APR for a personal loan?
In India (2025), a good APR for personal loans ranges from 10% to 18% for borrowers with excellent credit scores. Average APRs are 15-24%, while those with poor credit might see APRs of 24-36%. Always compare APRs from multiple lenders to get the best deal.
Can I negotiate APR with lenders?
Yes, APR is often negotiable, especially if you have a good credit score, stable income, or existing relationship with the bank. You can negotiate by comparing offers from multiple lenders, asking for fee waivers, or leveraging your credit worthiness.
What fees are included in APR?
APR typically includes interest charges, processing fees, loan insurance, administrative fees, legal fees, and valuation fees. It does NOT typically include late payment penalties or prepayment charges.
