Understanding Debt-to-Income Ratio

Your Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio is a personal finance measure that compares an individual's monthly debt payment to their monthly gross income. It is one of the key factors lenders use to determine your ability to repay a loan.

What is a Good DTI Ratio?

  • 36% or less: Excellent. Lenders view you as a low-risk borrower.
  • 37% - 43%: Good/Manageable. You may still qualify for loans, but terms might be stricter.
  • 44% or higher: High Risk. You may have trouble getting approved for new credit.

How to Lower Your DTI

To improve your DTI ratio, you can either increase your income or decrease your debt. Paying off high-interest credit cards or refinancing loans can help lower your monthly debt obligations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does DTI affect my credit score?
Directly, no. Credit bureaus do not know your income, so DTI is not part of your credit score calculation. However, high debt utilization (which affects DTI) does impact your score.
What debts are included in DTI?
DTI includes monthly payments for mortgages, car loans, student loans, credit cards, and other personal loans. It typically does not include expenses like groceries, utilities, or taxes.
