Complete Guide to RD Calculator 2025

A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a popular savings scheme in India that allows you to deposit a fixed amount every month for a pre-determined tenure. It offers interest rates similar to Fixed Deposits (FDs) but with the flexibility of monthly contributions.

How RD Interest is Calculated?

In India, RD interest is compounded quarterly by most banks. The formula used is complex because each monthly installment earns interest for a different duration.

M = P * n + Interest

Where:
M = Maturity Amount
P = Monthly Installment
n = Number of months
Interest is calculated on each installment compounded quarterly.

Benefits of Recurring Deposit

  • Disciplined Savings: Encourages a habit of saving regularly.
  • High Returns: Offers interest rates comparable to FDs (6% - 8%).
  • Low Entry Barrier: Start with as low as ?500 per month.
  • Safe Investment: Backed by DICGC insurance up to ?5 Lakhs.

RD Taxation Rules 2025

Interest earned on RD is fully taxable as per your income tax slab. TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) at 10% is applicable if the interest exceeds ?40,000 in a financial year (?50,000 for senior citizens).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Can I miss an RD installment?

If you miss an installment, banks may charge a penalty (usually ?1.50 per ?100 per month). Continuous defaults may lead to closure of the RD account.

2. Is RD interest taxable?

Yes, RD interest is fully taxable. It is added to your total income and taxed according to your slab. TDS is also applicable.

3. Can I withdraw RD before maturity?

Yes, premature withdrawal is allowed but attracts a penalty (usually 1% lower interest rate). You will get interest only for the period the deposit was held.

4. What is the minimum tenure for RD?

The minimum tenure for a bank RD is usually 6 months, and the maximum can go up to 10 years.
