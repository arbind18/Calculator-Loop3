Frequently Asked Questions

What is a moratorium period in education loans?
A moratorium period is a grace period during which students are not required to repay their education loan. This typically includes the entire duration of the course plus an additional 6-12 months after course completion. During this time, students can focus on their studies and job search without the burden of EMI payments. However, interest may still accrue during this period depending on the loan terms.
How does simple interest capitalization work during moratorium?
When you choose simple interest capitalization, the interest accrued during the moratorium period is added to your principal loan amount. This means your effective loan amount increases after the moratorium ends. For example, if you borrow ₹10 lakhs at 9.5% for a 4-year course, approximately ₹3.8 lakhs of simple interest will be added to your principal, making your effective loan ₹13.8 lakhs. Your EMI calculations will then be based on this higher amount.
What is the maximum tenure for education loan repayment?
Most banks in India offer education loan repayment tenures ranging from 5 to 15 years, with some premier institutions extending up to 20 years for large loan amounts. The tenure typically depends on the loan amount, your income after graduation, and the lender's policies. Longer tenures result in lower monthly EMIs but higher total interest paid over the loan period. It's advisable to choose a balanced tenure that keeps your EMIs manageable while minimizing interest costs.
Can I prepay my education loan without penalties?
Yes, most education loans in India allow prepayment without any penalties or foreclosure charges. Many students start making partial prepayments as soon as they secure employment to reduce their interest burden. Some banks even offer incentives for timely prepayments. Prepaying even small amounts during the moratorium period or early in your repayment tenure can significantly reduce your total interest outgo and loan tenure.
Is collateral required for education loans?
Collateral requirements depend on the loan amount. For loans up to ₹7.5 lakhs, most public sector banks in India do not require collateral under government schemes. For loans between ₹7.5 lakhs and ₹15 lakhs, banks may accept parental income proof or a third-party guarantee instead of collateral. Loans above ₹15 lakhs typically require collateral in the form of property, fixed deposits, or other tangible assets. However, policies vary across lenders and some private banks offer collateral-free loans up to higher amounts based on the institution and course.
What expenses are covered under education loans?
Education loans typically cover tuition fees, hostel fees, examination fees, library charges, laboratory fees, purchase of books and equipment, travel expenses for study abroad programs, study tour expenses, project work costs, laptop/computer purchases, caution deposits, building funds, and other expenses related to completing the course. Some banks also finance travel insurance and initial living expenses for overseas education. The coverage may vary between lenders, so it's important to clarify the scope with your bank before applying.
How to calculate education loan eligibility?
Education loan eligibility depends on multiple factors including the course and institution (premier vs. non-premier), academic performance, co-applicant's income and credit score, existing financial obligations, and collateral availability. Generally, banks sanction loans covering 80-100% of total education costs. Your family's income capacity, typically assessed through the Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR), determines how much EMI you can afford post-graduation. Most banks require the post-study EMI to be within 40-50% of expected future income.
