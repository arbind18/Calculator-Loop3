Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical interest rate for personal loans in India?

Personal loan interest rates in India range from 10.5% to 24% per annum depending on your credit score, income, employment type, and lender. Banks offer 10.5-16% for high credit score (750+) borrowers with stable income. NBFCs charge 12-20% with faster approval but higher rates. Fintech lenders range 14-24%, especially for lower credit scores or first-time borrowers. Key factors: CIBIL score above 750 gets best rates (2-4% lower), salaried employees get 1-2% better rates than self-employed, existing bank customers may get 0.5-1% discount, and loan amount impacts rate (higher amounts often get lower rates). Always compare 3-4 lenders before applying.

How much personal loan can I get based on my salary?

Personal loan eligibility is typically 10-20 times your monthly salary, subject to maximum limits. For salaried: ₹25,000 salary = ₹2.5-5 lakh loan, ₹50,000 salary = ₹5-10 lakh, ₹1 lakh salary = ₹10-20 lakh. Banks use FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) - your total EMIs shouldn't exceed 50-60% of monthly income. Example: If monthly income is ₹75,000 and existing EMIs are ₹15,000, available FOIR is ₹30,000 (40% of income), allowing ₹5-7 lakh new loan at 14% for 5 years. Higher credit score, longer job tenure (2+ years), working with reputed companies, and no existing loans significantly increase eligibility. Self-employed need minimum ₹4-5 lakh annual income and 2+ years business vintage.

What are the processing fees and hidden charges in personal loans?

Processing fees range 1-3% of loan amount (₹2,000-5,000 minimum). On ₹5 lakh loan: expect ₹5,000-15,000 processing fee plus 18% GST. Additional charges: prepayment penalty 2-5% (if foreclosed before 6-12 months), late payment charges ₹500-750 per missed EMI, bounce charges ₹400-600 for failed auto-debit, documentation charges ₹500-1,000, stamp duty 0.1-0.2% (state-specific), and some lenders charge loan cancellation fees ₹500-1,500. Hidden costs: insurance bundling (optional but pushed aggressively), higher rates for quick disbursal, and reducing rate calculation instead of flat rate can make effective interest 1.7-1.9x higher. Always check APR (Annual Percentage Rate) which includes all fees - it's 2-3% higher than quoted interest rate. Negotiate processing fee waiver during festive offers.

How does prepayment save interest on personal loans?

Prepaying personal loans saves massive interest because interest is calculated on reducing balance. Example: ₹5 lakh loan at 15% for 5 years has EMI ₹11,895 (total interest ₹2.14 lakh). If you prepay ₹1 lakh after 1 year, you save ₹88,000 in interest and finish 18 months early. Early prepayment saves most: prepaying ₹50,000 in year 1 saves ₹45,000 interest vs only ₹15,000 if prepaid in year 4. Strategy: Make annual lump sum payments from bonus/tax refunds, or add ₹2,000-5,000 extra to monthly EMI - on ₹5 lakh loan, extra ₹3,000/month reduces tenure from 60 to 42 months, saving ₹65,000. Check prepayment penalty: most banks allow free prepayment after 6-12 months. Zero prepayment penalty loans are best for aggressive repayment plans. Use EMI calculator to see exact savings before prepaying.

What documents are required for personal loan approval?

Essential documents for salaried: Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, passport), address proof (utility bills, rental agreement), last 3 months salary slips, 6 months bank statements showing salary credits, Form 16 or ITR for last 2 years, employment proof (offer letter, ID card, experience letter), and passport photos. For self-employed: Business registration documents (GST, shop act, incorporation certificate), ITR with computation for 2-3 years, audited financial statements (balance sheet, P&L), business bank statements 12 months, ownership proof of business premise, and TDS certificates if applicable. Additional: Existing loan statements, rent agreement if renting, and some banks require guarantor for loans above ₹10 lakh. Digital loans: Only Aadhaar, PAN, bank statements and selfie for amounts up to ₹2-3 lakh. Processing time: Digital approval in 2 hours to 2 days, traditional banks take 3-7 days. Incomplete documents are main reason for rejection.

How to improve credit score for better personal loan terms?

Credit score 750+ gets best rates and instant approval. To improve: Pay all EMIs, credit card bills on time (35% weight in score - even one 30-day delay drops score 50-80 points). Keep credit utilization below 30% (using ₹90,000 of ₹3 lakh limit is better than maxing out ₹50,000 limit). Maintain old credit accounts (15% weight - 5-year-old credit card helps more than new one). Mix of credit types helps: having credit card + loan shows better management than only cards. Check report quarterly for errors: 1 in 4 reports have mistakes - dispute them immediately. Limit loan applications: each enquiry drops score 5-10 points; apply to maximum 2-3 lenders within 15 days (counted as single enquiry). Build credit history: if no credit, get secured credit card against FD, use for 6 months. Timeframe: significant improvement takes 6-12 months of disciplined behavior. Score 650→750 can save ₹2-3 lakh on ₹10 lakh loan via 3-4% lower interest rate.

When should I consider personal loan balance transfer?

Balance transfer makes sense when new lender offers 2-3% lower interest rate and you have substantial tenure left (minimum 2+ years, ₹2+ lakh outstanding). Example: ₹4 lakh outstanding, 3 years left at 18%, transfer to 14% saves ₹48,000 (after ₹8,000 processing fee). Best time: After 12-18 months of good repayment history when credit score improves, or during festive offers when banks aggressively compete. Calculate break-even: Processing fee ÷ monthly interest saving = months to recover cost. Process: New lender pays off existing loan, issues fresh loan with better terms. Check: Current lender's foreclosure charges (usually 2-4%), new lender's processing fee (negotiate waiver), any lock-in period, and whether you can top-up (additional loan amount). Risks: Restarting tenure increases total interest, multiple transfers hurt credit score. Alternative: Negotiate with current lender first - show competing offer, they often match rates to retain you, saving transfer hassle.
